Notes for: Thomas ANDERSON, SR

Enlisted in the War of 1812 in Mercer Co, KY. It was said that he was in the
Battle of Chalmette, generally known as the battle of New Orleans, and
that he saw the Enligh commander Sir Edward Pakenham fall.

After the battle of New Orleans, 8 Jan. 1815, Thomas Anderson walked from
New Orleans to Mercer Co, Kentucky, only to find that his family had moved
to Dubois Co, IN.

On 15 Dec, 1822, Thomas Anderson was commissioned a Lieut. Col. in the
Indiana State Militia that protected southern Indiana at that time. He wore
white trousers, a blue coat, with sword, belt, scabbard and epaulettes. His
widow sold the uniform to Samuel Postlewait, Sr. of Ireland, IN.

His grave at Hillsboro Cem. is unmarked, and to the immediate right of
his father's.
