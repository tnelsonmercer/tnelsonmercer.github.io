Notes for: Jacob SKELTON, SR


Stone in Providence Cem. near N.W. corner reads:
In memory of
Jacob Skelton, Sr.
died
Sept. 23, 1853
In the 85th year
of his age.

From "John Skelton of Georgia" at South Bend Public Library:
Jacob and brother, John, are in Pendleton Co, SC, in 1800 census, while
the rest of the family is across the Savannah River in Elbert Co, GA.
In about 1803, the SKELTON/SCALES family removed to Warren Co, KY.
In late 1807, they moved across the Ohio River into then Knox (now Gibson)
County, Indiana Territory. There, Jacob and his brothers James and Robert,
after participation in the Battle of Tippecanoe, remained until death.

From "History of Gibson Co, Indiana":
Jacob Skelton, Sr, and his brothers, Robert and Z. Skelton, came and
located in Gibson Co in 1810. The most prominent of the brothers was
Jacob, who was for many years a justic of the peace. He settled some
twelve miles outhest of Princeton. Jacob opened the first store in the
southeastern part of hte county in 1829.
