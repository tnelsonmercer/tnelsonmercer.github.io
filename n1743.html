Notes for: William ANDERSON, SR

From "The Anderson Family of the Irish Settlement," by George R. Wilson:
It is claimed that both Willim and his father, Gilbert, served in the American
Revolutionary War. After William and Jane married, they lived near the
junction of the Alleghany and Monogahela Rivers, then moved to
Mercer Co, KY, where several of their children were born. During the
War of 1812, William moved his family to Ireland, Dubois Co, IN,
reaching their destination on May 7, 1815. His sons William, Jr, and
Andrew, had come to Dubois Co. in the fall of 1814 and built a log
cabin. William, Sr, purchased 160 acres in Dubois Co, IN, with son
Thomas, on 6 Nov. 1815. Here began the "Irish Settlement."

1820 Census of Bainbridge Twp, Dubois Co, IN: William Anderson, Sr, head of
family consisting of two men age 16-26; one man age 26-45; one man over 45;
two girls age 10-16; one woman age 16-26; and one woman over 45. His son
Thomas Anderson is listed as a separate family; a man age 26-45 and a woman
age 16-26.

1830 Census of Bainbridge Twp, Dubois Co, IN: William Anderson, Sr, head of
family of one man age 60-70; one woman age 20-30, and one woman age 60-70.

William Anderson, Sr, did not appear to apply for pension or for bounty land from
his service in the American Revolution. The name William Anderson does not
appear on the Indiana list showing those drawing a pension in pursuance of the
Act of Mar 18, 1818.

William and father Gilbert were recruited in August, 1781, into Capt. Robert Miller's
Fourth Company, Sixth Battallion, of Col. James Taylor's Regiment, from their
home in Coleraine Township, Lancaster County, Pennsylvania.

William, Sr, spent his last days from December 1835, until his death, April 22, 1836,
at the home of his son John Anderson.
