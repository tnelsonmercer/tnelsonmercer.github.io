Notes for: Simpson S DYE


Simpson enlisted for three years as a private in Company C, 58th
Regiment in the Civil War. At that time he was 5' 7.5" tall, light
complexioned with gray eyes and he listed his occupation as a
carpenter.

During the Battle of Murfreesboro, or Battle of Stoneriver in Tennessee,
on the 31st day of December 1862, he was wounded by a fragment of a
shell on the left hand and three weeks later the army surgeon amputated
a portio of his arm just above the wrist. He was honorably discharged
because of this disability on April 18, 1863, and returned to Owensville,
Indiana.

We worked at making hand-made furniture for some time in Fort Branch,
Indiana, but in May of 1898, he applied for a pension.
